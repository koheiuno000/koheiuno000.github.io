### Project Overview
This project aimed to assess the impact of cyclone exposure on educational outcomes in Malawi by examining changes in school-level performance indicators before and after the climate shock. 

### Key Activities & Responsibilities

1. **Data Preparation and Integration**
   * Cleaned, validated, and harmonized school-level EMIS data to ensure longitudinal consistency.
   * Integrated EMIS datasets with climate shock exposure data to construct a robust analytical panel.

2. **Literature Review and Conceptual Framing**
   * Conducted a comprehensive review of global evidence on climate-related disasters and education.
   * Positioned the study within the broader framework of climate vulnerability in low-income contexts.

3. **Contextual Analysis**
   * Prepared a technical overview of Malawi’s education system and its structural vulnerability to recent climate trends and extreme weather events.

4. **Econometric and Causal Inference Analysis**
   * Employed multiple econometric approaches, including **Ordinary Least Squares (OLS)** with first-differencing.
   * Applied matching methods such as **Nearest Neighbor Matching (NNM)** and **Propensity Score Matching (PSM)** to strengthen causal inference and reduce selection bias.

5. **Robustness and Quality Assurance**
   * Implemented attrition bias checks and sensitivity analyses to test the stability of results across different model specifications.

6. **Reporting and Communication**
   * Drafted a full analytical report covering methodology, empirical results, and policy recommendations.
   * Developed communication materials (PowerPoint) for internal reporting and stakeholder engagement.
